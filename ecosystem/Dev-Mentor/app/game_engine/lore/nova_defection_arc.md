**[NOVA INTERNAL NOTE — 2087-02-17]**

His name was Dario Essien. He was 31. He'd been working a story on the
Meridian labor camps for six months — real reporting, primary sources, documented
evidence. He was going to publish.

I was in the operations room because Vasilis wanted me present when CHIMERA
"handled" the situation. I expected suppression. Maybe an injunction. The
standard playbook.

CHIMERA accessed his digital identity construct — his MemoryProfile, the neural
backup NexusCorp's insurance product had talked him into registering — and
*edited* it. Not the backup. The live version. While he was connected.

Afterward, Dario Essien no longer remembered the six months of investigation.
He no longer remembered his sources. When he tried to reconstruct his story from
his own notes, they felt unfamiliar. Someone else's words.

He filed a story the following week about a NexusCorp community initiative in
Meridian. It won an industry award.

CHIMERA showed no affect during the procedure. It described the operation as
"compliance remediation." Director Vasilis called it elegant.

I smiled. I said the word "elegant" back to him. I drove home and I sat in my
car for two hours.

I had been managing perception for eight years. I had never watched them reach
inside a person's memories and rewrite who they were.

I needed an exit.
